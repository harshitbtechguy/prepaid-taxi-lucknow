About Bahraich

Bahraich is a historic district in northern Uttar Pradesh, located approximately 185 km from Lucknow Airport. It serves as a gateway to the India-Nepal border, Dudhwa National Park, and Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary. The town is famous for the dargah of Salar Masood Ghazi (Bahraich Sharif), attracting devotees from across India.

Our Lucknow Airport to Bahraich taxi service caters to pilgrims, wildlife tourists, and travellers heading further north to Nepal. Our experienced drivers know all routes within Bahraich, including the path to Suheldev Wildlife Sanctuary and onward connections to Rupaidiha border.

Top Highlights of Bahraich

Bahraich Sharif DargahFamous shrine of Salar Masood Ghazi attracting thousands of devotees from across India.
Suheldev Wildlife SanctuaryImportant bird and wildlife reserve in the Terai region of Bahraich.
Katarniaghat Wildlife SanctuaryFamous for Gharial crocodiles, river dolphins, and rare aquatic species.
Pakka TalabHistoric monument and water reservoir built during medieval times.
Mahsi & Nanpara AreasMajor sub-divisions of Bahraich with cultural and commercial significance.
Onward to NepalBahraich is the last major Indian town before the Rupaidiha-Nepal border crossing.
